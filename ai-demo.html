We believe in transparency about what PULSE can and cannot do

When PULSE Works Well

Clear signal patterns

When multiple pharmacy indicators spike simultaneously, our predictions are very accurate (92% sensitivity)

Regular seasonal outbreaks

For predictable patterns that follow historical trends, PULSE provides valuable 2-3 week warnings

Urban areas with pharmacy coverage

Where our pharmacy network has good geographic coverage, signal detection is reliable

Supporting human decision-making

PULSE excels as a decision support tool for health authorities, not as a replacement

Honest Limitations

New serotypes or mutations

Novel dengue strains without historical patterns are harder to predict accurately

Rural areas without pharmacies

Our model requires pharmacy data; areas without coverage have limited prediction capability

Confounding signals

Flu season or other febrile illnesses can create false positives in our early warning system

Not a diagnostic tool

PULSE predicts outbreak probability, not individual infections. It complements, not replaces, clinical diagnosis
